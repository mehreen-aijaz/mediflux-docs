The **Customers** section is your central hub for managing everyone your pharmacy sells to. This includes individual consumers, businesses, and other pharmacy stores linked through MediFlux. From adding a new customer profile to tracking outstanding dues and managing billing notes, everything you need to maintain accurate customer accounts is here.

---

## Overview

Customer profiles connect to your sales workflow so staff can quickly look up accounts, record payments, and review past purchases and outstanding balances, all without leaving MediFlux.

---

## What you can do in Customers

- **Maintain a customer directory:** store contact details, GSTIN, license numbers, and ratings for every customer
- **Track outstanding payments:** see at a glance how much each customer owes you and the total across all customers
- **View sales history:** every sale is linked to the customer it was made to
- **Record payments:** mark dues as paid and keep your ledger accurate
- **Manage billing notes:** record and track credit notes issued to customers and debit notes raised by connected retailers
- **Accept or reject store link requests:** connect with other pharmacy stores on MediFlux as linked customers
- **Export customer data:** download customer data as PDF or Excel for accounting and audits

## Customer types

MediFlux distinguishes between two types of customers:

**Linked Stores** are other pharmacy stores on the MediFlux platform that you've connected with as a customer. These are typically used when you supply to or transact with another pharmacy. These connections start as a Store Request, which you can accept or reject.

**Local Customers** are individual consumers or businesses you add manually. These are your regular walk-in customers, regular buyers, or local businesses.

When adding a local customer you can choose between two profiles:

**Consumer:** for individual walk-in patients. Supports fields like age, gender, Aadhaar number, and PMJAY/Ayushman Bharat scheme details.

**Business:** for businesses or other pharmacies you supply to. Supports fields like business name, license number, and GSTIN.

You can filter your customer list by type at any time using the **Linked Stores** and **Local Customers** quick-filter chips.

## Store Requests

The **Store Requests** page shows incoming connection requests from other pharmacy stores on the MediFlux platform who want to link with you as a customer. Once accepted, the store appears as a Linked Store in your Customer List and you can start transacting with them seamlessly.

## Trash

Deleted customers aren't gone forever. The **Trash** page holds all customer profiles that have been removed from your system, so you can restore them if needed. View deletion history, check outstanding balances at the time of deletion, and restore customers back to your active directory with one click. All linked sales records and ledger entries are preserved even after deletion.

## Common mistakes to avoid

<Warning title="Forgetting to add the Opening Outstanding">
If a customer already owes you money before you add them to MediFlux, make sure to enter it in the **Opening Outstanding** field when creating their profile. This field cannot be edited after saving. If you forget, correct it through the customer's <a href="/docs/customers/ledger">Ledger</a> using an **Adjustment entry**.
</Warning>

<Warning title="Skipping GSTIN and License Number for business customers">
These fields are optional but highly recommended for business customers. Without GSTIN your sales entries will not be GST-ready, which can cause issues when generating GSTR reports.
</Warning>

## Frequently asked questions

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="What is the difference between a Linked Store and a Local Customer?">
    A Linked Store is another pharmacy on MediFlux that has connected with you through a Store Request. A Local Customer is anyone you add manually, whether individual consumers or businesses. Both appear in your Customer List and work the same way once added.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I add both Consumer and Business profiles?">
    Yes. When adding a new customer you choose whether they are a Consumer or a Business. Each type has its own set of fields suited to that customer type.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens to sales history if I delete a customer?">
    All sales records and ledger entries are preserved even after a customer is deleted. You can still view them from the Trash page via **View Sales**. Restore the customer at any time to bring them back to your active list.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I track how much all my customers owe me at once?">
    Yes. The Customer List page shows a summary card with the total outstanding amount across all customers. The Ledger page also shows a running total at the bottom of the customer panel.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is the PMJAY/Ayushman Bharat checkbox mandatory?">
    No. It is optional. Tick it only if the customer is a registered PMJAY beneficiary. It does not apply any automatic discount. It simply tags the customer so their sales can be handled separately during billing and tracked in PMJAY sales reports.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
