The **Modules** tab lets you turn optional features on or off for your store. If a feature doesn't apply to your pharmacy, you can disable it here to keep MediFlux focused on what you actually use.

---

## Available modules

| Module | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Clinic Module** | Enables medical features like patient records and prescriptions |
| **Accounting Module** | Enables accounting features like journal entries, ledgers, and reports |
| **Estimate Bills** | Enables the estimate bill feature, for creating quotations and estimates |

Each module shows its current status — **Enabled** or **Disabled** — along with a button to toggle it.

## Enabling or disabling a module

Click **Enable** or **Disable** next to a module to change its status. A confirmation prompt appears before the change is applied:

> **Are You Sure?**
> Are you sure you want to enable [Module Name]?

Click **Confirm** to proceed, or **Cancel** to back out.

<Figure
  src="/images/modules-confirmation-prompt.png"
  title="Confirmation Prompt"
  description="Are you sure you want to enable or disable this module?"
/>

<Note>
Disabling a module hides its related features across MediFlux, but doesn't delete any existing data tied to it. For example, disabling the Clinic Module hides the Clinic section from your sidebar, but your doctor and appointment records aren't lost.
</Note>

---

## FAQs

**What happens to my data if I disable a module?**
Your existing data isn't deleted. Disabling a module only hides the related features from your sidebar and workflows until you re-enable it.

**Is the Clinic Module required to use MediFlux?**
No, it's optional. If your pharmacy doesn't run a clinic, you can leave it disabled.
