When a patient books an appointment themselves using your **Public Booking Link**, it doesn't get added directly to your Appointments list. Instead, it lands here as a **Pending Booking**, waiting for you to review and approve or reject it.

---

## Searching pending bookings

The search bar lets you search by **patient name or phone**. A **1 Pending** counter at the top shows how many booking requests are awaiting action, and the refresh icon reloads the list.

## Booking request cards

Each pending booking is shown as a card with:

| Field | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Patient name and phone** | The patient who made the booking |
| **Doctor** | The doctor the booking is requested with |
| **Date** | Requested appointment date |
| **Time** | Requested time slot |
| **Symptoms** | Symptoms entered by the patient while booking |

*[Screenshot: Booking request card]*

---

## Approving or rejecting a booking

Each card has two actions:

| Action | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Approve** | Confirms the booking, adding it to your Appointments list |
| **Reject** | Declines the booking request |

<Note>
Once a booking is approved, it moves to the **Appointments** page. Bookings left pending don't get scheduled automatically — they need action from you.
</Note>

---

## FAQs

**Where do pending bookings come from?**
They're created when a patient books an appointment through your **Public Booking Link**, shared from the Appointments page.

**What happens if I reject a booking?**
The booking request is declined and does not appear in your Appointments list.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Leaving booking requests unattended, patients booking through the public link expect a timely response, so review pending bookings regularly
- Approving a booking without checking doctor availability, since these are patient-submitted requests, double-check the requested date and time don't conflict with existing appointments
